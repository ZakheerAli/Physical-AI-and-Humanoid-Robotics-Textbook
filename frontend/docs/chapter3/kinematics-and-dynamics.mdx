# Kinematics & Dynamics

Kinematics and dynamics are two fundamental branches of mechanics that are crucial for understanding and controlling robot motion. Kinematics describes the motion of robots without considering the forces that cause it, while dynamics deals with the relationship between forces, torques, and the resulting motion. This chapter will introduce the basic concepts of both kinematics and dynamics as they apply to robotics.

## Kinematics

Kinematics is essentially the geometry of motion. It focuses on the spatial configuration of a robot manipulator and its movement from one position to another, without considering the forces or moments causing the motion.

### Forward Kinematics

Forward kinematics is the process of calculating the position and orientation of the robot's end-effector (or any other point of interest) given the values of its joint variables (e.g., joint angles for revolute joints, joint displacements for prismatic joints).

### Inverse Kinematics

Inverse kinematics is the reverse problem: given the desired position and orientation of the end-effector, calculate the required joint variables. This is generally more complex than forward kinematics and can have multiple solutions, no solutions, or unique solutions depending on the robot's configuration and workspace.

Solving inverse kinematics often involves:
*   **Analytical Solutions:** Possible for simpler robots or specific configurations.
*   **Numerical Solutions:** Iterative methods used for complex robots (e.g., Jacobian-based methods).

## Dynamics

Dynamics is the study of motion and the forces that cause it. In robotics, dynamics allows us to understand how forces and torques applied to a robot affect its acceleration, velocity, and position. This is essential for control, trajectory planning, and interaction with the environment.

### Newton-Euler Formulation

This method applies Newton's second law and Euler's equation for rotational motion recursively from the base to the end-effector (forward recursion) and then back from the end-effector to the base (backward recursion) to calculate joint forces/torques.

Understanding kinematics and dynamics is foundational for designing, simulating, and controlling robotic systems. These principles enable engineers to predict robot behavior, plan complex movements, and ensure stable and efficient operation.
